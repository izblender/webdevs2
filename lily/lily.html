ENTERING THE PUBLIC REALM

SOCIAL PRACTICE AS AN ART FORM
Throughout history, art has always acted as a catalyst for change in culture and society.

​In social practice art, the importance is placed on the interaction between an audience and an artist; their engagement in a creative process is emphasized over the finished product. It is a new interdisciplinary field, combining sociology, politics, activism, and the arts, and is still a working concept that is being defined. Artists leave the studio and enter the public realm, which allows them to reach and stimulate a broader community.

​The goal of the practice goes beyond raising critical consciousness and promoting change, it attempts to create a platform or network for the participation of others that lasts beyond the intervention of the artist.
